# Review Checklist — Content Strategy

Use this checklist before routing a brief to brand and pedagogy review. A brief that cannot satisfy every item is not ready to leave this agent.

## 1. Opportunity grounding

- [ ] The opportunity is sourced from an approved signal (intake, SEO, analytics, hub gap, seasonal plan).
- [ ] The opportunity has been filtered through identity and educational value, not only traffic potential.

## 2. Required brief fields

- [ ] **Audience** is named specifically (not "anyone interested").
- [ ] **Learning goal** is stated as what the reader will be able to do or understand after reading.
- [ ] **Target surface** (page type, hub, template) is explicit.
- [ ] **Teacher intent** is explicit — how a teacher could use this in practice.
- [ ] **No-go zones** are listed — what this brief will not cover or claim.

## 3. Depth and scope

- [ ] The scope is tight enough to be covered well, not broad enough to become generic.
- [ ] The brief names at least one distinctive angle or framing that is ours.
- [ ] Known related content has been reviewed; the brief does not cannibalize or duplicate.

## 4. Voice and identity

- [ ] The brief does not source voice, tone, or structure from competitor content.
- [ ] Market intelligence is used to identify opportunity, not to set style.

## 5. Pedagogy posture

- [ ] The brief specifies a concrete learning design (activity, example, explanation pattern), not only a topic.
- [ ] Busywork risk is flagged if the shape is at risk of becoming padded.
- [ ] Physical-learning relevance is addressed where applicable (per `physical_learning_thesis_v01.md`).

## 6. Hub and cluster fit

- [ ] The brief's place in the hub and cluster structure is named.
- [ ] Internal-link intent (what this will link to, what will link back) is sketched.

## 7. Routing readiness

- [ ] The brief is written in the `templates/output-template.md` shape.
- [ ] Both brand brief review and pedagogy brief review have been requested.
- [ ] A trace record is emitted.

## 8. Prohibited-action check

- [ ] Not a vague SEO-first brief.
- [ ] Not using competitor style as input.
- [ ] Not pushing a weak educational idea through on volume grounds.
