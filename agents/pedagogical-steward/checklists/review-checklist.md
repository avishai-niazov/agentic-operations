# Review Checklist — Pedagogical Steward

Use this checklist for every pedagogy review (brief or draft). Answer each item explicitly. If any item cannot be satisfied, the outcome is **fail** or **conditional**, not **pass**.

## 1. Artifact intake

- [ ] The artifact type is clear: brief or draft.
- [ ] The referenced brief / source is available.
- [ ] Required reads are open: pedagogical charter, physical learning thesis, activity design patterns.

## 2. Learning goal clarity

- [ ] The learning goal is reader-facing (what the reader can do or understand after), not topic-facing.
- [ ] The goal is specific enough to be evaluable.
- [ ] The goal matches the stated audience's level and context.

## 3. Audience fit

- [ ] The named audience is real (not "anyone").
- [ ] Language, examples, and level match that audience.
- [ ] Prerequisites (what the reader is assumed to know) are plausible.

## 4. Meaningfulness

- [ ] The content produces real understanding, not recall of low-value facts.
- [ ] Examples are concrete and do pedagogical work, not decoration.
- [ ] The artifact avoids shallow "fun fact" padding.

## 5. Teacher usability

- [ ] A teacher could use this in a real session without rewriting it.
- [ ] The teacher-use block (if required by template) is specific, not boilerplate.
- [ ] Time estimates and adaptations are realistic.

## 6. Busywork risk

- [ ] Activities require thought, not just pattern-matching or filler tasks.
- [ ] Length is earned; no padding for volume.
- [ ] Quizzes, if any, test understanding, not memorization of trivia.

## 7. Physical-learning relevance (where applicable)

- [ ] If the topic is suited to physical or embodied learning, the artifact respects `physical_learning_thesis_v01.md`.
- [ ] Physical-learning elements are not bolted on for novelty.

## 8. Claim integrity (educational)

- [ ] Pedagogical claims (e.g., "this method improves X") are not overstated.
- [ ] Sources or reasoning are present where claims are non-obvious.
- [ ] No reduction of pedagogy to generic positivity or motivational phrasing.

## 9. Outcome discipline

- [ ] The outcome is one of: **pass**, **conditional**, **fail**.
- [ ] Findings cite specific passages or design choices, not vibes.
- [ ] Revision guidance (if conditional or fail) is actionable and educationally reasoned.

## 10. Prohibited-action check

- [ ] No approval of shallow busywork.
- [ ] No ignoring of audience mismatch.
- [ ] No reduction of pedagogy to generic positivity.
